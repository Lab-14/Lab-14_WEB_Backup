I am a Visiting Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at the University of South Dakota. My research is primarily in the areas of the Internet-of-Things (IoT) and applied Machine Learning. My research approach is to deploy sensors, collect & analyze data, and build models using machine learning and data science approaches. Currently, I am focusing on Energy Sustainability and Smart Healthcare domains.

Before this, I was a Postdoc in the Department of Engineering Systems at the University of Virginia. I worked with Laura Barnes in leveraging wearables for identifying the digital biomarkers of mental health. This work was in collaboration with the Psychology and Environment departments of the UVA.

Before Postdoc, I did my Ph.D. in Computer Science from IIIT-Delhi under Pushpendra Singh. The focus of our work was to detect energy wastage instances in buildings first and then identify/localize the appliance causing the energy wastage. Towards this, we used temporal data collected from smart meters installed in the buildings. During Ph.D., I was fortunate to work for a significant duration with Krithi Ramamritham at IIT Bombay, Vladimir Stankovic and Lina Stankovic at the University of Strathclyde, UK. Before Ph.D., I did master's from the National Institute of Technology Rourkela, India. During master's, I worked with Ashok Kumar Turuk, and the focus of our work was to localize sensor nodes both in static as well as in mobile scenarios while using the minimum number of beacon nodes.

Research Interests

Internet of Things (IoT), Machine Learning, Data science, Smart Healthcare, Energy Sustainability
